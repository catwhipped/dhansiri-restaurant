Mughal Inspirations

In the 16th and 17th centuries the Mughal Empire swept across the Indian subcontinent, bringing with it a royal courtly cuisine. Legendary emperors like Akbar and Shah Jahan dined on elaborate feasts featuring aromatic rice, tender meat and fragrant spices. Bengal, a wealthy province of the empire, embraced these influences while adding local flavours of mustard, chilli and river fish.

Food & Festivities

Food plays a central role in Bengali celebrations. From the colourful Pohela Boishakh new year feast to the sweet treats of Durga Puja, meals bring family and friends together. Traditional sweets such as rosogolla, mishti doi and sandesh mark special occasions. Savoury snacks like puchka and chotpoti are enjoyed at street-side stalls, while elaborate rice and curry spreads grace wedding tables.
