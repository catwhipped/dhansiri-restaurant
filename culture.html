Discover Bengali Culture & Cuisine

Uncover the rich history and traditions behind the flavours we serve.

Origins of Bengali Cuisine

The Bengali culinary tradition dates back centuries and has been shaped by the fertile lands and rivers of the Bengal delta. Fresh fish, fragrant rice and seasonal vegetables form the foundation of this regional cuisine. Spices such as mustard seed, cumin, coriander and turmeric infuse each dish with vibrant colour and aroma. Many recipes were perfected in family kitchens and passed down through generations.

Mughal Inspirations

During the Mughal Empire, Bengal became a centre of trade and culture. The Nawabs of Bengal adopted Persian and Central Asian cooking techniques, introducing luxurious ingredients like saffron, dried fruits and nuts. Biryanis and rich meat stews, simmered for hours with yoghurt and ghee, showcase this influence. Today, Bengali dishes like kacchi biryani, beef tehari and shahi haleem reflect a harmonious blend of local and Mughal traditions.

Food & Festivities

Food plays a central role in Bengali celebrations. From the colourful Pohela Boishakh new year feast to the sweet treats of Durga Puja, meals bring family and friends together. Traditional sweets such as rosogolla, mishti doi and sandesh mark special occasions. Savoury snacks like puchka and chotpoti are enjoyed at street-side stalls, while elaborate rice and curry spreads grace wedding tables.

Experience the Flavours

Now that you know the story behind our dishes, taste them for yourself. Our menu features a variety of appetizers, mains and desserts inspired by Bengali heritage. From fiery curries to delicate bhortas, there's something for every palate.
